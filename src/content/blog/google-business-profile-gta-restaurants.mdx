## What Is Google Business Profile and Why Should You Care?

Google Business Profile (GBP) is the free listing that appears when someone searches for your restaurant on Google or Google Maps. It shows your name, address, hours, photos, reviews, menu link, and more. It's the single most important piece of digital real estate your restaurant owns.

Here's why: most restaurant discovery now starts on Google. When someone searches for a restaurant, your GBP is usually the first thing they see — often before they ever visit your website.

If your GBP is incomplete, outdated, or unoptimized, you're handing customers to your competitors. This guide walks through everything you need to know to set up and optimize your GBP for maximum visibility across the GTA.

## Setting Up Your Profile: The Foundation

If you haven't claimed your GBP yet, start here. Go to [business.google.com](https://business.google.com) and search for your restaurant. If it already exists (Google often creates listings automatically from public data), claim it. If not, create a new one.

**Verification** usually requires a postcard mailed to your business address, though phone and email verification are sometimes available. This takes 5-14 days. Don't skip it. An unverified profile has minimal visibility.

### Essential Information to Complete

**Business name:** Use your exact legal business name. Don't stuff keywords in here. "Tony's Pizza" is correct. "Tony's Pizza - Best Pizza in Toronto - Wood Fired - Italian Restaurant" will get your profile suspended.

**Address:** Your exact street address. If you're in a food court or shared space, include the unit number. If you deliver but don't have a storefront, you can set a service area instead.

**Phone number:** Use a local GTA number (416, 647, 905, 289). Avoid call tracking numbers as your primary number since they can cause citation inconsistency.

**Website:** Link to your main website. If you have a dedicated location page for a multi-location restaurant, use that specific URL.

**Hours:** Keep these accurate. Nothing frustrates a potential customer more than showing up to a closed restaurant that Google said was open. Update for holidays, special events, and seasonal changes. Google now lets you set special hours in advance.

## Choosing the Right Categories

Categories are the highest-impact optimization in your entire GBP. Google uses them to decide which searches your restaurant appears for.

**Primary category:** Choose the most specific option available. Here's the hierarchy from most to least effective:

- "Halal Restaurant" is better than "Restaurant" (if you're halal)
- "Sushi Restaurant" is better than "Japanese Restaurant" (if sushi is your focus)
- "Brunch Restaurant" is better than "Cafe" (if brunch is your main draw)

**Secondary categories** (you can add up to 9): Add every relevant category. A pizza restaurant that also does catering and has a bar should have:

- Primary: Pizza Restaurant
- Secondary: Italian Restaurant, Catering Food and Drink Supplier, Bar, Takeout Restaurant, Delivery Restaurant

### GTA-Specific Category Tips

The GTA has unique cuisine diversity. Use it to your advantage:

- If you serve halal food, add "Halal Restaurant" as a category. This term gets over 22,000 monthly searches in the GTA
- "Caribbean Restaurant" and "Jerk Chicken Restaurant" are separate categories. If both apply, add both
- "Poke Restaurant" and "Sushi Restaurant" are different. Pick the one that matches your core offering

## Attributes: The Details That Convert

Google lets you add attributes that appear as badges on your profile. Customers filter by these, so completeness matters.

**Service options:** Dine-in, takeout, delivery, curbside pickup, no-contact delivery. Check every one that applies.

**Dining options:** Breakfast, lunch, dinner, dessert, catering, seating. Add all that apply.

**Amenities:** Wi-Fi, outdoor seating, parking, wheelchair accessible, family-friendly, live music. These show up as filters in Maps searches.

**Crowd and atmosphere:** Casual, cozy, trendy, upscale. Pick what matches.

**Dietary options:** Vegetarian, vegan, gluten-free, halal, kosher. If you offer any of these, add them. These are high-intent filters that customers actively use.

**Payment methods:** Debit, credit, cash, contactless payments. List them all.

## Photos and Videos: Show, Don't Tell

Your GBP photos are often the deciding factor between a customer choosing your restaurant or scrolling past. Profiles with rich, frequent photo uploads consistently outperform sparse ones — Google's own help docs flag photos as a ranking and engagement signal.

### What to Upload

1. **Cover photo:** The single best photo of your restaurant. Usually a signature dish or an inviting interior shot
2. **Logo:** Your current logo, clean and recognizable
3. **Exterior photos:** Front entrance from the street (helps people find you), signage, patio if you have one
4. **Interior photos:** Dining room, bar area, private dining, any unique features
5. **Food photos:** Your top 10-15 dishes, photographed well with natural lighting. No filters
6. **Team photos:** Kitchen staff, front of house, the owner. Makes your business feel human
7. **Videos:** 30-second clips of food being prepared, the dining atmosphere, or a quick tour

### Photo Best Practices

- **Upload new photos at least weekly.** Freshness matters for Google's algorithm
- **Minimum resolution:** 720x720 pixels. Higher is better
- **Natural lighting only.** Dark, blurry photos do more harm than good
- **No text overlays, watermarks, or collages.** Google's guidelines prohibit these and may remove them
- **Monitor customer photos.** Customers upload their own photos to your profile. If any are unflattering or inaccurate, you can flag them for removal

## Google Posts: Free Marketing on Google

Google Posts are short updates that appear directly on your profile. Think of them as social media posts that show up in Google Search and Maps results.

### Post Types

- **What's New:** General updates, announcements, new menu items
- **Events:** Upcoming events with date, time, and description
- **Offers:** Promotions with start and end dates
- **Products:** Highlight specific menu items with photos and descriptions

### Posting Strategy for GTA Restaurants

**Post 2-3 times per week.** Here's a weekly template:

- **Monday:** This week's special or featured dish with a photo
- **Wednesday:** Behind-the-scenes or team spotlight
- **Friday:** Weekend event, live music announcement, or reservation reminder

### What Makes a Good Google Post

- **Lead with a photo.** Posts with photos get 10x more engagement
- **Keep text under 300 characters.** Short, direct, actionable
- **Include a call-to-action button.** "Order Online," "Call Now," "Learn More," or "Book"
- **Use relevant keywords naturally.** "Our new lamb tagine is the perfect halal dinner option in Scarborough" includes keywords without feeling forced

Posts expire from prominent placement after 7 days, so consistency beats perfection. A decent post every few days outperforms one perfect post per month.

## Review Management: The Reputation Game

Reviews are the second most important ranking factor for Google Maps after your GBP information. They're also the number one factor in whether a new customer chooses you over the restaurant next door.

### Getting More Reviews

**Make it frictionless.** Create a short link to your Google review page:

1. Search for your restaurant on Google
2. Click "Write a review" on your own profile
3. Copy the URL
4. Create a shortened link with a service like bit.ly

Put this link on:

- Table cards or tent cards
- Receipts
- Follow-up texts after reservations
- Your website
- Email signatures

**Ask at the right moment.** The best time to ask for a review is immediately after a positive experience. Train your staff to recognize when a table is happy and mention the review link naturally.

**Set a target.** Aim for 5-10 new reviews per month, minimum. Consistency matters more than volume in any single month.

### Responding to Reviews

**Respond to every review within 24 hours.** Google tracks response rates and speed.

**For 5-star reviews:**
- Thank them by name
- Reference something specific about their experience if they mentioned it
- Invite them back for a specific reason ("Come try our new weekend brunch menu")

**For negative reviews:**
- Acknowledge the issue without being defensive
- Apologize sincerely
- Offer to resolve it offline ("Please reach out to us at [email] so we can make this right")
- Keep it professional. Potential customers will read your response

**Never:**
- Offer incentives for reviews (violates Google's policies)
- Ask for only positive reviews (also a violation)
- Argue with a negative reviewer publicly
- Use a template for every response (it looks automated and insincere)

## Common Mistakes GTA Restaurants Make

After auditing dozens of GBP profiles across the GTA, here are the most common problems we see:

1. **Wrong primary category.** Using "Restaurant" instead of a specific cuisine category. This is the highest-impact fix for most restaurants
2. **Inconsistent hours.** Especially holiday hours. Google downgrades profiles that show incorrect hours based on user reports
3. **No photos updated in months.** Google treats a stale profile as a potentially closed business
4. **Ignoring the Q&A section.** Customers ask questions on your GBP. If you don't answer, other users (or Google's AI) will answer for you, sometimes incorrectly
5. **Duplicate listings.** Multiple GBP profiles for the same location confuse Google and split your review count
6. **Keyword stuffing the business name.** This gets your profile suspended. Use your real business name, nothing more
7. **Not using attributes.** Missing halal, vegetarian, or patio attributes means you're invisible to customers filtering by those features
8. **Linking to a generic website.** For multi-location restaurants, each GBP should link to that location's specific page, not just the homepage
