## Why TikTok Matters for Your Restaurant

Let's get straight to the point: for Gen Z diners, social media has overtaken Google, Yelp, and word of mouth as the way they find new restaurants. And TikTok is the platform driving the biggest share of that discovery.

In Toronto specifically, the food scene on TikTok is massive. Hashtags like #TorontoFood have billions of cumulative views. Videos of restaurants in the GTA regularly break 100K views, sometimes millions. And unlike Instagram, TikTok's algorithm doesn't care how many followers you have. A restaurant with 200 followers can get the same reach as one with 200K if the content hits.

That's the opportunity. TikTok is the most democratic platform for restaurant marketing right now. You don't need a massive following, a professional camera crew, or a big budget. You need a phone, good food, and a posting strategy.

## Content Types That Actually Work

Not all restaurant content performs the same on TikTok. After producing content for restaurants across the GTA, here are the formats that consistently drive engagement and foot traffic.

### Behind-the-Kitchen Content

People love watching food being made. There's something satisfying about seeing raw ingredients transform into a finished dish, and TikTok's format is perfect for it.

What to film:

- **Prep sequences:** Chopping, marinating, assembling. Speed it up 2-3x for a satisfying watch
- **The cook:** Flames, sizzling pans, steam. Anything with motion and sound
- **Plating:** The final arrangement. Clean, deliberate, and satisfying
- **The reveal:** Start with raw ingredients, end with the finished dish

**Tip:** Film in vertical (9:16). Shoot close-up. Let the food fill the frame. The sounds of cooking often perform better than any music you could add.

### Staff Personality Videos

Your team is your brand's personality. The restaurants that blow up on TikTok almost always feature their people, not just their food.

Ideas:

- **"A day in the life" of a chef or server.** Start early morning, show the chaos of prep, the rush of dinner service, the quiet close
- **Team challenges.** "Can our new cook make [signature dish] in under 5 minutes?"
- **Staff reactions** to trying a new menu item for the first time
- **The owner's story.** Why did you open this restaurant? What's the origin story? People connect with real stories from real people

### Trending Audio and Formats

TikTok rewards you for using trending audio and participating in popular formats. You don't need to chase every trend, but jumping on the right one with a food angle can push your content to a much wider audience.

How to find trends:

- Check the TikTok Creative Center for trending sounds
- Watch what other food accounts are posting
- Look at what's on your For You page in the food niche

**The key:** Adapt the trend to your restaurant. Don't force it. If a trend doesn't naturally fit, skip it. The wrong trend feels cringe. The right one feels effortless.

### Customer and Community Content

- **Customer reactions** to their first bite (ask permission, obviously)
- **"Wait for it" videos** where the surprise is the food arriving at the table
- **Street interviews** outside your restaurant. "Have you tried [restaurant name]?" This format performs very well in Toronto neighbourhoods
- **Neighbourhood content.** Show what it's like in your area. Link your restaurant to the vibe of your neighbourhood

## Posting Strategy: Frequency and Timing

### How Often to Post

**Minimum: 3 times per week.** This is the floor. TikTok's algorithm favours accounts that post consistently. If you can do 5-7 times per week, even better. But consistency beats volume. Posting 3 times every week is better than posting 7 times one week and then disappearing for two weeks.

### Best Times to Post for GTA Restaurants

Based on when food content performs best in the Toronto timezone:

- **Tuesday-Thursday:** 11:00 AM - 1:00 PM (lunch crowd browsing)
- **Friday:** 4:00 PM - 6:00 PM (weekend dinner planning)
- **Saturday-Sunday:** 10:00 AM - 12:00 PM (brunch and weekend plans)
- **Daily:** 7:00 PM - 9:00 PM (evening scroll time)

These are starting points. After a month of posting, check your TikTok analytics for when YOUR specific audience is most active and adjust.

### Content Batching

Here's a practical workflow that most restaurant teams can manage:

1. **Pick one day per week** for filming. Ideally during prep or a slower service period
2. **Film 5-8 raw clips** in 30-60 minutes. Different dishes, angles, and moments
3. **Edit and schedule** throughout the week. Most clips need minimal editing on TikTok. Add text, pick a sound, done
4. **Post on schedule.** Use a content calendar to stay consistent

You're not building a production studio. You're capturing moments that already happen in your restaurant every day. The filming itself should take less than an hour per week.

## Hashtag Strategy for Toronto Restaurants

Hashtags help TikTok categorize your content and show it to the right audience. Use a mix of broad and local tags.

### Broad Food Tags (high volume)

- #FoodTikTok
- #RestaurantTikTok
- #FoodPorn
- #CookingTikTok
- #EatWithMe

### Toronto/GTA Specific Tags (targeted reach)

- #TorontoFood
- #TorontoEats
- #TorontoRestaurants
- #GTAFood
- #MississaugaFood
- #BramptonEats
- #TorontoFoodie
- #EatInToronto

### Cuisine-Specific Tags

Use tags specific to your cuisine type. Examples:

- #HalalFood #HalalToronto
- #SushiToronto #JapaneseFood
- #CaribbeanFood #JerkChicken
- #ItalianFood #PastaTikTok
- #BrunchToronto #TorontoBrunch

### How Many Hashtags

**Use 3-5 hashtags per post.** Don't stuff 30 hashtags in your caption. TikTok's algorithm doesn't reward hashtag volume like Instagram used to. Pick the most relevant ones and keep it clean.

## Equipment: Your Phone Is Enough

I want to be direct about this because I've seen restaurant owners delay starting on TikTok because they think they need professional equipment. You don't.

**What you actually need:**

- **Your phone.** Any phone from the last 3-4 years shoots good enough video. iPhone or Android, doesn't matter
- **Natural light or kitchen lighting.** Your restaurant already has lighting. Use it. Near a window is ideal for food shots
- **A cheap phone mount or tripod.** Low-cost gear is enough to stabilize your shots. Get one with flexible legs that can attach to shelves or equipment
- **TikTok's built-in editor.** It has text, music, effects, speed control, and transitions. You don't need CapCut or Premiere Pro to start

**What you don't need:**

- A professional camera
- Ring lights (unless your space is very dark)
- Expensive editing software
- A production team

Start with your phone. Post 10 videos. See what works. If you're getting traction and want to level up the production quality, invest then. Not before.

## Common Mistakes Toronto Restaurants Make on TikTok

### Posting Only Promotional Content

"Come try our new special!" every single post gets old fast. TikTok is entertainment first, marketing second. If your content isn't entertaining, informative, or satisfying to watch, people scroll past.

**The ratio:** For every 1 promotional post, create 4 posts that are pure value or entertainment. Show the food being made. Share a cooking tip. Feature your staff. Make people feel something.

### Inconsistent Posting

Posting 5 times in one week, then nothing for three weeks, then 3 posts, then nothing for a month. TikTok's algorithm rewards consistency above almost everything else. An account that posts 3 times every week will outperform one that posts 15 times in one week and goes dark.

### Ignoring Comments and Engagement

When someone comments on your video asking "Where is this?" or "What dish is that?" and you don't respond, you're leaving money on the table. Every comment is a signal to TikTok's algorithm that your content is engaging. Responding to comments within the first hour of posting boosts your reach significantly.

Better yet, **reply to comments with a new video.** This is one of TikTok's most powerful features. Someone asks what your best dish is? Film a 15-second reply showing that dish being made. It's new content, it's engagement, and it often performs better than the original video.

### Watermarked Reposts from Instagram

Taking your Instagram Reels (with the Instagram watermark) and posting them on TikTok is something the algorithm actively penalizes. If you're creating content for both platforms, film natively or remove watermarks before cross-posting.

### Over-Polished Content

TikTok is not Instagram. Overly polished, heavily edited, professional-looking content often underperforms compared to raw, authentic footage. The platform rewards content that feels real and spontaneous. A shaky phone video of a sizzling pan with natural kitchen sounds will outperform a perfectly lit, color-graded food commercial.

## Measuring What Works

After your first month of posting, dig into your TikTok analytics (available on Business or Creator accounts). Look at:

- **Views per video:** What's your baseline? Which videos break above it?
- **Average watch time:** Are people watching the full video or dropping off in the first second?
- **Profile visits:** Are views converting into people checking out your profile?
- **Follower growth rate:** Steady growth means consistent content is working
- **Comments:** Quality of engagement matters more than quantity

The metrics that matter most for restaurants are **profile visits** and **website clicks** (if you have a link in bio). These indicate people who are actually considering visiting your restaurant, not just passively watching food content.

## Getting Started This Week

If you've never posted on TikTok for your restaurant, here's your first-week plan:

1. **Day 1:** Set up a TikTok Business account. Add your restaurant name, logo, location, and a link to your website or Google Maps listing in your bio
2. **Day 2:** Film 3 clips during your next service. One food prep shot, one finished dish reveal, one team member moment
3. **Day 3:** Post your first video. Keep it simple. Trending audio + food close-up + 2-3 hashtags
4. **Day 5:** Post your second video. Try a different format from your first
5. **Day 7:** Post your third video and check analytics on your first post

That's it. Three posts in your first week. No pressure to go viral. Just start the habit of capturing and posting consistently.
